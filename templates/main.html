CAD

Among the many projects I have done, one of the projects I carried out was our High School Building’s second floor design. Having a goal to provide a “Makerspace” for our school’s high school student and to encourage our school’s STEM field subjects and activities, the school wanted CAD students to create a design in which the professional architecture can understand what it should look like. From a group of 15 students in the CAD Class, my friend and I were picked and asked to carry out this renewal after the director, principal and so on all saw our initial proposal and design. Both my partner and I went to ask our peers about what they would want the new second floor to look like and the features they would want to include. It took six whole months to get to our final design and to get our design to be passed for construction. Within the six months, my partner and I provided more than ten visits and presentations to the higher officials of our school - each time with a new design. The following video shows the making of the final design that was constructed over the summer break of 2017 and it was fortunate for me to have the opportunity to apply my learning to real life and to actually bring change to the community.
